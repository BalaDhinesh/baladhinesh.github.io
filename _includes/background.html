Bio

I am a Senior Undergraduate Student in Electrical Engineering with Minor in Computing at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras, India.

My interests broadly include the fields of Hardware Accelerators, Computer Architecture, Hardware-Software Co-design, and FPGA Architecture.

My current research as part of my Bachelor's Thesis is on Binary Neural Network Inference on FPGAs under Prof. Nitin Chandrachoodan, IIT Madras and in collaboration with KLA Advanced Computing Labs. I am exploring various FPGA architectures and algorithms, particularly for the U-Net Convolutional Neural Network architecture, in order to alleviate the memory bottleneck problem for semantic segmentation models. I am also working with Prof. Cong "Callie" Hao at Sharc Lab, Georgia Institute of Technology on Approximate Computing for Graph Neural Networks. I replaced floating point multipliers in the graph convolutional networks with various INT8 and INT16 approximate multipliers and employed fine-tuned Quantization-aware training techniques to obtain similar accuracy results compared to FP32, which resulted in a significant reduction in area and power.
